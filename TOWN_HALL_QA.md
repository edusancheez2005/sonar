## Q1. In 30 seconds or less, give us an overview of what you've been doing.

- I've been building **Sonar** (sonartracker.io) — a crypto market intelligence platform
- Built it over the past ~8 months alongside my internship
- It uses **AI-driven analytics** to aggregate and analyse blockchain transactions, news, and sentiment
- The goal: help crypto traders make **profitable, informed trades** by providing reliable, transparent, AI-powered market data
- One dashboard, one place — cutting through the noise

## Q4. What key moments at Microsoft have influenced your work with Sonar?

- **Learning to prioritise and balance** — Emily and Anil taught me the "buckets" system; that's how I manage a full internship AND a startup. Dividing priorities properly means you *can* do both at a high level
- **Won 2 pitch competitions** (including King's College Idea Factory Finals) — those wins came directly from what I learned here
- **Storytelling over features** — Sneha and Richard taught me to tell the *why*, not just the *what*. That changed everything in my investor pitches. "The room feels different when you speak with belief"
- **Confidence speaking to people** — talking to customers and clients at Microsoft made me less scared of presenting. Being technical AND able to communicate is rare, and Microsoft pushed me to develop both
- **The culture** — people genuinely go out of their way to help you grow. That support gave me the confidence to put myself out there

## Q5. One piece of advice for folks in the room?

- **Don't be scared to start building**
- Everyone has time — you can always organise yourself to find it
- Life doesn't end at 5pm. You can get **1% better every day**
- Start planning your project, your dream — it doesn't have to be perfect, just *start*
- With AI, it's genuinely never been easier to build things — the barriers are lower than ever
- Start small, start messy — just **start**
